How to Probe
  1. Insert 3–4" deep at the edge of the root ball — not dead center, not touching the stem.
  2. For this weekend's transplants, that edge is where the original nursery soil meets the surrounding sand — that's the true test of whether water is reaching roots.
  3. Center-of-root-ball readings run falsely wet right after planting (still-saturated nursery mix) — don't probe there.
  4. Blueberries: probe only inside each plant's amended acid pocket, never the surrounding native sand.
  5. Firespike cutting: skip the soil probe entirely — check the rooting medium surface by feel/sight, no roots yet to read.
  6. Reading sits right on the 3/4 border? Pull the probe, move it an inch or two over (still at the root ball edge), and recheck. Still borderline? Default to GREEN/hold — safer to wait than to risk overwatering, especially for blueberries and coontie.
